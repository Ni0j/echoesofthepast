About
This collection reimagines New York City's manhole covers as Fulu—mystical talismans traditionally used in Chinese culture to seal, protect, and ward off harmful forces. These ancient symbols, once offering spiritual protection, find their modern counterparts in the iron covers marking our streets today. Acting as shields, they separate the bustling life above from the intricate networks below, ensuring safety and preventing calamities, much like the Fulu did in ancient times.
        Each chapter pairs these functional talismans with stories from ancient Chinese texts, presenting the original Chinese script alongside English translations. These narratives draw connections between the talismans' historical purposes and their contemporary forms, transforming the manhole covers into symbols of unseen guardianship, hidden in plain sight.
        The talismans featured are adaptations from historically documented Fulu, specifically the Seventy-Two House-Sealing Talismans, with their functions reinterpreted for modern contexts. These stories offer protection and provide insight into the cultural significance and historical power of seals.
