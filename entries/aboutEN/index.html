About
In this collection, it reimagine the manhole covers of New York City as Fulu—mystical talismans historically used in Chinese culture to seal, protect, and ward off malevolent forces. These ancient symbols, which once provided spiritual protection, find their modern counterparts in the iron covers that mark our streets today. Serving as shields, they separate the vibrant life of the city's surface from the hidden, intricate systems beneath. These modern-day seals protect the underground infrastructure, ensuring safety and preventing calamities in much the same way Fulu safeguarded the people of old.

Each chapter of this book pairs functional talismans with stories from ancient Chinese texts, illustrating their symbolic power. The book provide the original Chinese script alongside English translations, drawing connections between the talismans' ancient uses and their contemporary forms. In this way, the manhole covers become more than just part of the city's landscape—they stand as symbols of unseen guardianship, hidden in plain sight.

Moreover, the talismans included have been adapted from historically existing Fulu referenced from Seventy-Two House-Sealing Talismans, with their functionalities reinterpreted for modern use. These stories offer not only protection but insight into the cultural significance of seals and the power they've held throughout history.
